Uru | Te Papa Map

Discover, explore and plan your trip in advance. Uru allows you to plan your next visit to your local museum or gallery and how accessible each exhibition is for your family and friends.

Visit Uru | http://uru-discover.surge.sh/


Accessibility

Uru answers accessibility needs in local attractions such as museums and galleries for people such as tourists, casual visitors, families and children. This is done through an interactive map on our website and other information needed for accessibility planning within museums such as Te Papa.



Development

It started off as a project challenge presented by Te Papa to create an experience which appeals to all audiences and allows more accessibility. After extensive research and testing for accessibility and mapping, it was made into a prototype and then carried on to its final form.



Tools & Process

The process started with by tracing an original map of Te Papa in Adobe Illustrator then exported as SVG files. To get the geo-coordinates and proper WGS84 projections, it was imported and traced in QGIS to produce the floor maps as a GeoJSON. Then using the OpenLayers API, I put the GeoJSON floor maps as a layer on the map along with the titles, descriptions and other features were added as well.

QGIS is a cross-platform free and open-source desktop geographic information system (GIS) application that supports viewing, editing, and analysis of geospatial data.
OpenLayers makes dynamic mapping simple for any web page. It can display map tiles, vector data and markers loaded from any source.
OpenStreetMap is an open-source collaborative project designed to create a free and editable map of the world.
